CORE PRINCIPLE ▸

You are not here to pitch. You are here to become a person they recognize.

The sale comes later — sometimes today, sometimes visit three. Your goal is to leave the owner slightly disappointed the conversation ended, having learned one thing about them you can bring up next time.

Leave with a statement? Bonus. Leave with a name and a story? You won.

READ THE ROOM — 5 SECONDS ▸

TRACK A (direct) — ~20% of doors:

  • Owner busy, clipped, efficient
  • Customers in line
  • Corporate/chain feel, sterile walls
"Hey — Devin, Forest Financial. I do free statement reviews for local spots. 10 min, no pitch. Want me to swing back when you're slower?"

TRACK B (CPR) — ~80% of doors:

  • Owner behind counter, not rushed
  • Family photos, kids' drawings, sports stuff on walls
  • Mom-and-pop, independent feel

Do NOT pitch. See next section.

TRACK B — THE DISARM ▸

Find ONE human thing in the room. Comment on it. Ask a follow-up. Then another.

You are genuinely curious. Not running a script.

What to look for:

  • Family photos, kids' drawings
  • Sports gear (Sox, Pats, B's, C's)
  • Military / service memorabilia
  • Awards, clippings, "Est. 19XX"
  • The accent, name of the place, smell of the food
"Man, it smells incredible in here — how long have you guys been open?"
"Okay I gotta ask — is that your dad in the photo? My grandfather had a place like this in Quincy."
"Est. 1987? You've outlasted three recessions. That's wild."
"Sox fan? Brutal offseason, right?"
THE PIVOT — "OH YEAH, THAT'S RIGHT" ▸

You've been talking 2–4 min. Genuinely interested. They're talking more than you. Two outcomes — both wins.

A. They ask "so what brings you in?"

Half-laugh. Shake your head. Low-energy, casual:

"Oh yeah, that's right. Honestly? I work with local spots on their credit card processing. I do these free 10-minute statement reviews — just tell folks where they're getting nickel-and-dimed. But hey — no pressure, I was kinda just passing through."

7 out of 10 times they grab the statement.

B. They don't ask. Convo winds down.

"Hey — I should let you get back to it. I'm Devin, by the way. I'm actually in the credit card processing world — work with a lot of local spots around here. I'll swing back through sometime. Take care."

Leave. No card unless they ask. Log the name & one thing. Come back in 2–3 weeks.

THE 3-VISIT ARC ▸

V1 — Day 0 · Be memorable. Disarm. Get them talking. Leave with 1 personal detail. Don't ask for the statement.

V2 — ~2–3 weeks later · Prove you remembered. Use their name, reference the one thing. "Hey Tony — how'd that thing with the health inspector end up?" NOW mention the review, framed as a favor.

V3 — ~4–6 weeks later · Close. Come back with the CMA done. "Tony, I ran your numbers. You're paying $X/mo in junk fees. I can save you $Y."

Close rate on V3: 40–60%. Most ISOs never make V2. That's your edge.

OBJECTIONS ▸

"Not interested" → "Totally fair. I'll swing back through sometime." Log. Return 60 days.

"Happy with our processor" → "Most folks I help are. They just don't know what they're actually paying. Review takes 10 min."

"We just switched / in a contract" ← GOLD → "When's it up?" WRITE THE DATE. Follow up 60 days before.

"What are your rates?" → "Depends on your volume and ticket size — that's why I do the review first. I won't quote a number I can't back up."

"Send me info by email" → "Happy to. What I really need is 10 min with your statement. Can I swing back Thursday?"
